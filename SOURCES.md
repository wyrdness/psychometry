## Source Evaluation Notes

### Categories of Sources

**Primary Historical Sources**: Original 19th-century texts proposing psychometry (Buchanan, Denton) — valuable for understanding history of the claim, not evidence of validity.

**Scientific Research**: Controlled experiments consistently show null results. Parapsychology journals document testing failures.

**Psychological Research**: Robust evidence for cognitive biases (Barnum effect, confirmation bias, cold reading) that explain apparent psychometry.

**Skeptical Investigations**: Documented exposés of fraudulent techniques and failed controlled tests.

**Cultural/Entertainment**: Fiction and media perpetuate belief but provide no evidential support.

### Reliability Assessment

✓ **Highly Reliable**: Peer-reviewed psychology research, controlled experiments, replicable bias studies
✓ **Reliable**: Skeptical investigations with documentation, historical archives
⚠️ **Unreliable**: Anecdotal reports, testimonials, uncontrolled demonstrations
✗ **Not Evidence**: Fiction, entertainment media, promotional materials
